Narrative

Vehicle 1, operated by David Chen, was traveling northbound on I-95 in the center lane at an estimated 35 mph when it lost directional control and struck the concrete guardrail with the front driver-side quarter. Airbags deployed. The engine compartment sustained severe crush damage on impact. The vehicle was operating normally under its own power immediately prior to guardrail contact; the engine ceased operation after the collision. No evidence of pre-impact mechanical failure was observed at the scene.

Witness Statement — Marcus T. (independent)

"The car was driving normally, then it veered and hit the rail hard. The engine was clearly running right up until the moment of impact — it only died after it hit. It was the crash that killed the engine, not the other way around."

Officer Findings

Physical evidence (deployment, crush pattern, debris field) is consistent with a high-force collision event. Engine failure is attributable to impact damage to the oil pan and crankshaft, not to a pre-existing condition.
